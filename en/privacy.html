This Privacy Policy explains how OpenAlon ("OpenAlon", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, shares, retains, and otherwise processes information in connection with OpenAlon games, mobile applications, websites, support channels, live events, advertising activities, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

This Privacy Policy covers information processed through the Services themselves. Third-party platforms, app stores, payment processors, social networks, and external websites have their own privacy notices and policies.

1. Information We Collect

Depending on how you interact with the Services, we may collect the following categories of information:

  • Account and Profile Information: such as player ID, platform account identifiers, display name, login records, and support history.
  • Device and Technical Information: such as IP address, device identifiers, operating system, browser type, language settings, crash logs, and technical diagnostics.
  • Gameplay and Usage Information: such as session activity, progression, game events, interaction patterns, feature usage, referrals, and approximate region.
  • Purchase and Transaction Information: such as purchase receipts, subscription status, order identifiers, refund status, and entitlement history.
  • Communications and Community Information: such as support messages, moderation records, tournament or event submissions, and other content you submit through the Services.
  • Cookies, Advertising, and Measurement Data: such as cookie data, SDK signals, attribution data, ad identifiers, and analytics information used to understand performance and audience behavior.

2. How We Collect Information

We collect information in the following ways:

  • directly from you when you create a profile, contact support, make a purchase, participate in events, or otherwise interact with the Services;
  • automatically through gameplay, websites, applications, cookies, SDKs, logs, and similar technologies;
  • from platform providers, payment processors, app stores, analytics partners, advertising partners, fraud-prevention vendors, and customer-support tools.

3. How We Use Information

We may use information for the following purposes:

  • to provide, operate, authenticate, maintain, secure, and improve the Services;
  • to process purchases, subscriptions, entitlements, support requests, tournament participation, and live-service features;
  • to personalize content, difficulty tuning, offers, events, and player experiences where permitted;
  • to analyze usage, performance, retention, fraud signals, and campaign effectiveness;
  • to communicate with you about updates, support, policies, account matters, or promotional messages where allowed;
  • to protect our legal rights and comply with applicable law, regulation, legal process, or regulatory requests.

4. Legal Bases and Regional Processing Grounds

Where required by law, including in the EEA, United Kingdom, and Switzerland, we process personal data on one or more of the following grounds: performance of a contract with you, our legitimate interests in operating and improving the Services, your consent, and compliance with legal obligations.

Where consent is required for certain cookies, advertising, or tracking activities, you may withdraw that consent using the relevant in-service controls, device settings, browser settings, or consent tools made available to you.

5. Cookies, Advertising, and Analytics

We may use cookies, local storage, SDKs, pixels, and mobile advertising identifiers to remember settings, authenticate sessions, analyze traffic, measure campaigns, support attribution, detect abuse, and improve player experiences. Depending on your location, you may be able to control some of this processing through cookie banners, browser settings, device privacy controls, or in-app settings.

6. How We Share Information

We may disclose or make information available:

  • to hosting, infrastructure, engineering, moderation, anti-fraud, customer-support, analytics, and security providers acting on our behalf;
  • to app stores, payment processors, console platforms, tournament operators, or distribution partners needed to deliver the Services;
  • to advertising, attribution, and measurement partners where permitted by law and consistent with your choices;
  • where required by law, legal process, court order, or regulatory request;
  • to protect rights, safety, property, players, service integrity, or to investigate misuse, fraud, or security incidents;
  • in connection with a merger, acquisition, restructuring, financing, or sale of all or part of a business or assets.

7. International Transfers

The Services are intended for a global audience. Information may be processed in jurisdictions outside your country or region, including where we or our service providers operate. Where required, we use contractual, organizational, or other legally recognized safeguards for cross-border data transfers.

8. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary for service delivery, account administration, security, fraud prevention, analytics, dispute resolution, support, tax and accounting needs, and legal compliance, unless a longer or shorter period is required by law. We may de-identify or aggregate information so that it no longer identifies you.

9. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights relating to your personal data, such as the right to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability, or a copy of certain information. You may also have rights to withdraw consent, manage cookies or advertising settings, or appeal certain decisions.

If you reside in California or another U.S. state with privacy rights, you may have rights to know, access, correct, delete, and opt out of certain disclosures that could be considered "sale" or "sharing" under applicable law. To make a request, contact us using the details below. We may need to verify your identity and may limit or deny requests where permitted by law.

10. Children

We do not knowingly collect personal information from children where such collection would require parental consent under applicable law. If you believe that a child has provided information inappropriately, please contact us and we will review the matter and take appropriate action.

11. Security
